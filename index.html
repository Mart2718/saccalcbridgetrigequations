Signs by Quadrant (ASTC)
Q I — All positive
Q II — $\sin$ positive only
Q III — $\tan$ positive only
Q IV — $\cos$ positive only
General Solution Forms
$\sin$ & $\cos$: add $+ 2\pi n$
$\tan$ & $\cot$: add $+ \pi n$
$n \in \mathbb{Z}$ (any integer)
Useful Identity
Double-angle: $\sin 2x = 2\sin x \cos x$
Pythagorean: $\sin^2 x + \cos^2 x = 1$

Part 1 — Solve on $[0,\,2\pi)$
1
Isolate & solve
$2\cos x = \sqrt{3}$
Find all solutions on $[0, 2\pi)$.
Divide both sides by 2 first. Then ask: for what angle is $\cos = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$?
2
Isolate & solve
$\sqrt{3}\tan x + 1 = 0$
Find all solutions on $[0, 2\pi)$.
Isolate $\tan x$ first. A negative tangent means Q II or Q IV.
3
Square root — four solutions
$4\cos^2 x = 3$
Find all solutions on $[0, 2\pi)$. Expect four answers.
Divide to get $\cos^2 x = \frac{3}{4}$, then take $\pm$ square root. Two signs → four quadrants.

Find all real solutions. Use $+ 2\pi n$, $\;n \in \mathbb{Z}$.

5
General solution
$3\sin t = 0$
Find all real solutions. Use $+ \pi n$ or $+ 2\pi n$ as appropriate.
Where on the unit circle does $\sin = 0$? Think about the angle, not the quadrant.

Double-angle identity · factor
$\sin 2x = \sin x$
Find all solutions on $[0, 2\pi)$. Expect four answers.
Replace $\sin 2x$ with $2\sin x \cos x$, then move everything to one side and factor out $\sin x$.
